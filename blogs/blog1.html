Formal education

I began my formal education in 2003 in primary school 'Grbavica 2'. Ever since I was in primary school, I knew that my life goal was to become a programmer. I enjoyed playing video games and was always fascinated by the mechanisms that made them possible. I continued my formal education in the 'Third gymnasium' in Sarajevo, where I picked subjects that focused on math and programming. In 2015 I enrolled in the Faculty of Science at University of Sarajevo. After finishing 2 years and some disagreements with one of the professors I decided to take a break from it which ended up lasting 2 years during which I focused on learning web development. In 2020 I continued my college education.

Informal education

During my break from college, I decided to learn about web development. I started off by learning about JavaScript. Literature that I used was You Don't Know JS: by Kyle Simpson and Eloquent JavaScript by Marijn Haverbeke. Afterward, I started learning HTML from a book by Matthew MacDonald, HTML5: The Missing Manual and CSS from a book by David McFarland, CSS: The Missing Manual. At this point I was able to create my first website. I choose to create a website for an online game of Teamfight Tactics. A website I ended up creating can be found on Github .

Next natural step was to expand my knowledge by learning about some production-level tools for web development. Sass and React.js seemed interesting, so I choose those. I learned React.js from a book by Alex Banks and Eve Porcello, Learning React: Functional Web Development with React and Redux and Sass from online tutorials. Now that I have expanded my knowledge I decided to remake Teamfight tactics website with better coding practices. New website can be found here .

Now that I have "mastered" frontend it was time to move on to backend. After doing some research and due to my prior knowledge of C++, .Net was a natural choice. Books of choice for this were Pro C# 7: With .NET and .NET Core by Andrew Troelsen and Philip Japikse and Pro ASP.NET Core MVC 2 by Adam Freeman. First thing I did with my newly acquired skills was to create Portfolio .
