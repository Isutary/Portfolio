Getting started

After deciding to take a break from college I knew it was time to start looking for a job. Before I could do that I had to expand my skillset. After spending two months learning about web development I could start applying for jobs, not so much in hope of getting one as to get introduced to the whole job-seeking world.

First interview

The first interview I ever did was at a company called QSS. The position was a junior software developer. It was the first time I went to Vogosca so that alone was scary enough. I remember arriving half an hour early and being amazed by their workspace. The interview was very informal, we just sort of talked for a while, however, I focused on not being nervous.

After the interview, I called my brother about it and was very happy with how it all went. After the initial euphoria passed, I realized how little I knew and how much more there is still to learn. Maybe the best course of action was to try to get an internship.

Over the course of the next year, I widened my skill sets and applied to pretty much anything I could. Even though I didn't manage to get an internship or a job I was glad to see improvement in my interviewing skills and that I got much better at the technical parts of the interview.

First job

I was 'enjoying' Covid-19 lockdown when my brother called me to say that one of his friends is looking for a QA Engineer. It may not have been what I wanted to do, but I figured it would give me the opportunity to learn about production-level applications, so I gladly accepted. I have been working as a QA Engineer for the past 6 months, where my primary role was manual testing and QA of CMS for an online game of Rock-Paper-Scissors. I have also been responsible for automation testing of the same website. Through automation testing, I was introduced to Atata framework, selenium and some options for automation testing of RESTful APIs. Due to the nature of the job I had a lot of free time, which I invested in expanding my knowledge of web development. I also got familiar with agile development. I hope that I will soon be ready to start working as a junior software developer.
